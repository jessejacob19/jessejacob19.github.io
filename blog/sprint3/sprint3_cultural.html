What is Emotional Intelligence?

EQ has 5 categories:

Self awareness:

The ability to realise an emotion when it occurs. Being self aware allows you to evaluate your emotions and then manage them.

Self regulation:

Usually you don't have control when you experience emotions, but you can reduce the time that emotion lasts through techniques, such as reframing the situation. self regulation involves self control, trustworthiness, conscientiousness, adaptibility and innovation

Motivation:

This requires clear goals and a clear direction, achievement drive, commitment, innitiative and optimism

Empathy:

The ability to see how people feel is important to understanding and managing people when you can understand how a person feels you can interact in a more productive way.

Social skills:

Good interpersonal skills are key in life, it is very important for negotiating and influencing people.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is intelegence quotient which is derived from standerdised tests and focuses on mental ability alone. EQ or emotional intelegence is the ability to use your emotions and cognitive skills in your life.

Why is it important?

Emotional intelegence is important because we all deal with people and have to deal with ourselves and to be able to do that is a more efficiant and positive manner can make the struggles in life a lot easier.
